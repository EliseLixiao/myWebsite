In the summer of 2023, I had an memorable experience of studying and working in the neuroscience cohort led by Professor Gal Bitan, professor of Neurology at UCLA. I was part of an amazing cohort of supportive and inspiring peers who shared the same academic interests as me, with our enthusiastic PCA kindly helping me out along the way 😃

Seeing how memory loss haunted the lives of elderly people and their families during my summer visits to a local nursing home, I decided to propose a method of brain stimulation to potentially recover “lost” memories in patients with Alzheimer’s disease.

The problem before me was which form of brain stimulation I should choose, and how I should design the entire research. From the hours spent on paper reading, I learned that all of the past research in the field of memory retrieval has been done on animal models, especially rodents. Therefore, I was reluctant to propose using human subjects for fear that my inexperience would render the proposal impractical and an eventual failure, even though changing from rodents to humans was an interesting challenge I would like to conquer. On hearing my concerns, Professor Bitan encouraged me to go bold.

His encouragement completely changed my mind set during the rest of the program✊ I was thus able to fully concentrate on designing an innovative method of brain stimulation that’s as non-invasive and applicable to humans as possible. Even though going bold probably meant that my research proposal wouldn’t be accepted for clinical trials in the next few years before all the techniques involved become more mature, I never regretted making this choice. The challenge of my academic limits felt nothing compared to the overwhelming excitement brought by my exploration of a completely unknown world!
